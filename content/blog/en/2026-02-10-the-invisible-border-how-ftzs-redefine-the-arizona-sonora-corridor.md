## Introduction

The map says the border is at Nogales, but for savvy shippers in 2026, the border is invisible. As manufacturing shifts from Asia to Sonora, Mexico, the **Arizona-Sonora Corridor** has become the most critical trade route in North America.

At the heart of this boom is the **Foreign Trade Zone (FTZ)**—a legal "buffer zone" where your freight is technically outside of US Customs territory, even though it's sitting in a warehouse in Phoenix or Tucson. At CTM, we help our partners navigate this corridor to unlock massive financial benefits.

## The Financial Power of the 'Zone'

An FTZ isn't just a warehouse; it's a financial tool. If you are moving freight through the Arizona-Sonora corridor, using an FTZ provides three major advantages:

- **Duty Deferral:** You don't pay a cent in customs duties until the moment the freight leaves the zone for domestic consumption. This keeps cash in your business longer.
- **Duty Elimination:** If you import components into Arizona, assemble them, and then re-export them, you may never have to pay US duties at all.
- **Inverted Tariffs:** If the duty on a finished product is lower than the duty on its individual parts, you can choose to pay the lower rate when the item leaves the FTZ.
