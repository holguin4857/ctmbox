## The Visibility Blackout
Crossing the border at Nogales is a critical juncture for North American supply chains. However, the moment freight moves into Sonora, shippers using digital brokers often experience a sudden and complete visibility blackout. The GPS ping stops, communication goes dark, and supply chain managers are left completely blind for hours or even days.

Operating without visibility in the Arizona-Sonora corridor is a massive security risk, especially for mid-market shippers moving high-value inventory.

## The Flaw of Fragmented Networks
This visibility blackout occurs because asset-light brokers rely on deeply fragmented networks. When your load crosses the border, digital brokers frequently hand your freight off to disconnected, third-party carriers south of the checkpoint. 

Because these brokers do not own the trucks or the tracking hardware, they cannot provide unbroken telematics. They rely on third-party drivers to manually update an app. If that driver loses cell service, deletes the app, or hands the trailer off to yet another sub-contractor, your tracking goes entirely dark. A software platform cannot track a truck that is no longer on its network.
